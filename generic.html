I Designed and implemented a dashboard
for real-time tracking of budget allocations, releases, and expenditures.

The proposed budget dashboard for the Ministry of Finance, Budget, and National Planning provides a clear, visual summary of Nigeria’s 2024 actual budget performance. It is structured into four main sections: Overview, Revenue, Expenditure, and Deficit.

The OVERVIEW section displays key highlights including total actual revenues (₦1,011.46bn), expenditures (₦4,425.40bn), and the resulting fiscal deficit (₦118.13bn). These figures provide users with a quick understanding of how the budget is performing.

In the REVENUE section, income sources are categorized into oil and non-oil. The largest sources include Non-Oil Revenue (₦305.17bn), GOEs Retained Revenue, and Grants & Donor Funding (₦273.50bn each). Supporting visuals such as bar charts and pie charts help clarify the proportions of each revenue stream.

The EXPENDITURE section breaks down spending into categories like Non-Debt Recurrent Expenditure, Capital Expenditure, and Debt Service. Non-Debt Recurrent Expenditure accounts for the largest share at 46.96%, followed by Capital Expenditure at 25.61%. A pie chart visualizes the budget allocation.

The DEFICIT section focuses on how the ₦118.13bn shortfall is being financed. Financing sources include ₦29.65bn in new borrowings, ₦27.02bn from asset sales, and ₦2.18bn from privatizations. A circular diagram shows the distribution of these financing items. The dashboard’s purpose is to support transparency, improve budget monitoring, and aid in strategic fiscal planning. Its design enables both technical users and non-experts to easily interpret budgetary outcomes.

By integrating visual analytics and actual financial data, it provides decision-makers with actionable insights and strengthens public trust in financial governance. The dashboard ultimately promotes accountability, data-driven policy, and fiscal discipline across government institutions. .
